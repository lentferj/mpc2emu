# Disclaimer

## AI Assistance & Human Authorship

In the interest of transparency: mpc2emu was created by its **human author**
working together with Anthropic's **Claude**, an AI coding assistant.

**The ideas and the direction are human.** The concept of the tool, the project
vision, every feature (the multi-format conversion, the vintage resampling, the
ZuluSCSI CD/HDD output, append-to-existing-image, the Windows/pure-Python goal,
…), the priorities, and the design decisions all came from the human author.

**Claude assisted with the execution:**
- writing and refactoring the implementation code (parsers, writers, the
  EMU3 / EMU-fs and FAT16 filesystem builders, resampling, the CLI);
- analyzing binary file and disk formats from supplied reference data and hex
  dumps, and cross-referencing open-source documentation;
- drafting and maintaining the documentation.

**The reverse engineering rests on extensive hands-on human work** — the part no
AI can do:
- all testing and verification on **real E-mu E4XT hardware** (via ZuluSCSI);
  every "hardware-confirmed" result in this project came from the author loading
  banks on the actual instrument and checking that presets play;
- producing the **authoritative RE references** by formatting disks on the real
  hardware and supplying the images for analysis;
- **aural A/B comparison** of converted presets against the originals on real
  hardware, and providing the measurements that drove the fidelity work (LFO
  rates, filter behaviour, tuning, envelopes, …);
- deep **domain expertise** about the instruments and formats, and catching bugs
  through real-world use.

In short, the AI accelerated the coding and the format analysis, but the ideas,
the hardware reverse engineering, the listening tests, and the correctness of
the result are the product of substantial human effort.

## Hardware Risk

Loading untested files onto vintage sampling hardware carries an
inherent risk. Before using any file generated by mpc2emu on real
hardware:

1. **Test on a ZuluSCSI or SCSI2SD emulator** before connecting to
   irreplaceable equipment.
2. **Keep good, current backups of all your files** — and of every
   existing bank on the target device/media. mpc2emu writes raw disk
   images and can **modify existing images in place** (`--add-to`), so a
   mistake or bug could overwrite or corrupt data.
3. **Verify the output** with the `--info` flag and review all
   warnings before writing to hardware.

The authors accept **no responsibility** for data loss, hardware
damage, or any other adverse effects resulting from the use of this
software.

## SCSI Disk Images (.hda)

The EMU4 filesystem used in `.hda` images is proprietary and was
never officially documented by E-mu Systems. The on-disk structure
implemented here is derived from the open-source `emu3fs` kernel
module (GPL-2.0-or-later, David García Goñi,
<https://github.com/dagargo/emu3fs>).

While the superblock magic, version flags, and directory layout have
been verified against live hardware images, **compatibility cannot be
guaranteed across all firmware versions** of the EIV series.

Always test `.hda` images on a non-production ZuluSCSI setup first.
